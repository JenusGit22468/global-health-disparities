Implementation Framework

WHO-recommended 6-step approach for implementing mental health interventions in resource-constrained settings

1
Situation Analysis
Assess current mental health needs, resources, and barriers
2
Stakeholder Engagement
Build coalition with government, healthcare providers, and communities
3
Intervention Selection
Choose evidence-based interventions suitable for local context
4
Pilot Implementation
Test interventions in controlled settings with continuous monitoring
5
Scale-Up Planning
Develop strategies for broader implementation and sustainability
6
Evaluation & Adaptation
Monitor outcomes and adapt interventions based on evidence

Implementation Success Stories

Ethiopia
Primary Care Integration (mhGAP)

Trained 2,847 primary care workers in WHO mhGAP guidelines across 6 regions.

Results (2019-2023)
  • • 78% increase in mental health case detection
  • • 45% reduction in referral wait times
  • • $2.3M reduction in tertiary care costs
Brazil
Community Mental Health Centers

Established 2,650 psychosocial care centers (CAPS) integrated with family health teams.

Results (2017-2023)
  • • 67% reduction in psychiatric hospitalizations
  • • 85% patient satisfaction rate
  • • 290% increase in community-based care
Kenya
Digital Mental Health Platform

Deployed AI-powered screening and SMS-based interventions through mobile health network.

Results (2020-2024)
  • • 1.2M users reached in rural areas
  • • 42% engagement rate sustained
  • • 35% improvement in depression scores
India
Task Shifting (VISHRAM Program)

Trained 15,000 village health workers in brief psychological interventions for common mental disorders.

Results (2018-2024)
  • • 82% clinical effectiveness vs specialists
  • • $89 cost per DALY averted
  • • 156% increase in rural mental health access
