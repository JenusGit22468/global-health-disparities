Regional Analysis Methodology

Regional Classification

  • WHO Regions: Six official WHO regional groupings for global health analysis
  • World Bank Income Groups: Four-tier classification by Gross National Income per capita
  • Geographic Clusters: Sub-regional analysis for more granular insights
  • Economic Zones: Trade and development partnership considerations

Comparative Metrics

  • Treatment gap percentages standardized by population
  • Mental health workforce density per 100,000 population
  • Healthcare expenditure as percentage of GDP
  • Economic burden relative to national economic output

Data Integration

  • Multi-source validation: Cross-referencing WHO, World Bank, and IHME data
  • Population weighting: Adjusting for demographic differences across regions
  • Economic standardization: PPP-adjusted comparisons for fair assessment
  • Temporal alignment: Using most recent 3-year averages for stability

Performance Indicators

  • Critical: Treatment gap >85%, urgent intervention required
  • Poor: Treatment gap 70-85%, significant improvements needed
  • Moderate: Treatment gap 50-70%, progress being made
  • Good: Treatment gap <50%, strong performance maintained
