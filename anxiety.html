Key Research Insights

Gender Disparity: Women in LMICs are 90% more likely to experience anxiety disorders but face 50% greater barriers to accessing specialized care due to stigma and economic constraints.
Comorbidity Challenge: 68% of anxiety cases present with comorbid depression, yet only 15% of LMICs have integrated treatment protocols for dual diagnoses.
Early Onset Impact: Peak onset occurs in late adolescence (15-19 years), but average time to first treatment in LMICs is 11.2 years, resulting in significant educational and economic losses.
Primary Care Potential: Countries with anxiety screening in primary care show 65% higher detection rates and 45% better treatment outcomes compared to specialist-only models.
