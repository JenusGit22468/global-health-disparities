Key Research Insights

Criminalization Impact: Countries with punitive drug policies show 40% higher treatment gaps and 60% higher relapse rates compared to those with harm reduction approaches.
Medication-Assisted Treatment: Only 23% of LMICs have access to opioid substitution therapy, despite WHO recommendations and evidence of 75% reduction in overdose deaths.
Injection-Related Infections: 85% of new HIV cases among people who inject drugs occur in LMICs, yet needle exchange programs exist in only 42% of affected countries.
Economic Burden: Every $1 invested in evidence-based substance use treatment returns $7.10 in reduced healthcare costs, crime, and increased productivity.
